# San Diego Family Court Violation Filing Forms

## Overview
This directory contains the official court forms required for filing a violation in San Diego Family Court. These forms are used when one party has violated an existing court order (e.g., custody, visitation, or support orders).

## Required Forms for Violation Filing

### 1. **D-046** - Ex Parte Application and Order (San Diego Specific)
- **Purpose**: Request emergency orders or immediate court action
- **When to use**: When immediate relief is needed due to violation
- **Key sections**:
  - Type of relief requested (TRO, custody/visitation, order shortening time)
  - Notice requirements to opposing party
  - Declaration of urgency
  - Evidentiary declarations requirement

### 2. **FL-300** - Request for Order
- **Purpose**: Primary form for requesting court orders
- **When to use**: For all non-emergency order requests related to violations
- **Key sections**:
  - Child custody and visitation
  - Child support
  - Spousal support
  - Attorney fees
  - Property issues
- **Attachments**: Often requires FL-311 (custody), MC-030 (declarations)

### 3. **FL-305** - Temporary Orders
- **Purpose**: Request temporary orders while main case is pending
- **When to use**: When immediate temporary relief is needed
- **Key sections**:
  - Temporary custody/visitation
  - Temporary support
  - Temporary property orders

### 4. **FL-335** - Continuance of Hearing
- **Purpose**: Request to postpone a scheduled hearing
- **When to use**: When more time is needed to prepare or serve documents
- **Key sections**:
  - Current hearing information
  - Reason for continuance
  - Proposed new hearing date

### 5. **FL-410** - Order to Show Cause and Affidavit for Contempt
- **Purpose**: Formal contempt proceedings for violation of court orders
- **When to use**: When someone has willfully violated a court order
- **Key sections**:
  - Specific order violated
  - Details of violation
  - Request for sanctions/jail time
  - Supporting declaration

### 6. **FL-411** - Order to Show Cause (Contempt)
- **Purpose**: Court's order requiring party to appear and explain violation
- **When to use**: After FL-410 is filed and approved
- **Key sections**:
  - Hearing date/time
  - Specific allegations
  - Potential consequences

### 7. **FL-150** - Income and Expense Declaration
- **Purpose**: Financial disclosure for support calculations
- **When to use**: When requesting/modifying support or attorney fees
- **Key sections**:
  - Employment information
  - Income details
  - Monthly expenses
  - Assets and debts

### 8. **MC-030** - Declaration
- **Purpose**: Written statement of facts under penalty of perjury
- **When to use**: To provide detailed facts about the violation
- **Key sections**:
  - Factual narrative
  - Supporting evidence references
  - Signature under penalty of perjury

## Filing Process for Violations

### Step 1: Determine Type of Violation
- Custody/visitation violation
- Support payment violation
- Property order violation
- Restraining order violation

### Step 2: Choose Appropriate Track

#### Emergency (Ex Parte) Track:
1. Complete **D-046** (Ex Parte Application)
2. Complete **FL-300** (Request for Order)
3. Attach **MC-030** (Declaration with detailed facts)
4. File immediately, hearing within 24-48 hours

#### Regular Track:
1. Complete **FL-300** (Request for Order)
2. Attach relevant forms:
   - **FL-150** (if financial issues)
   - **MC-030** (declaration of facts)
3. Serve opposing party
4. Hearing in 3-6 weeks

#### Contempt Track (Most Serious):
1. Complete **FL-410** (Contempt Affidavit)
2. Court reviews and issues **FL-411** if approved
3. Personal service required
4. Criminal-level proceedings

### Step 3: Supporting Documentation
Always include:
- Copies of violated orders
- Evidence of violation (emails, texts, photos, receipts)
- Witness declarations (MC-030)
- Police reports (if applicable)

## Form Completion Tips

### General Requirements:
- Use black or blue ink
- Print clearly or type
- Do not leave required fields blank
- Sign under penalty of perjury
- Keep copies of everything

### San Diego Specific:
- Check correct courthouse division box
- Include department number if known
- Follow local rules (SDSC Rules)
- Use mandatory San Diego forms (D-046)

## Service Requirements

### Personal Service Required:
- FL-410/411 (Contempt)
- Ex parte applications (same day)

### Regular Service (Mail OK):
- FL-300 (16 court days before hearing)
- FL-305
- MC-030 (with main motion)

### No Service Required:
- FL-335 (if agreed)
- FL-150 (exchange with other party)

## Important Deadlines

- **Ex Parte**: Notice opposing party by 10 AM day before
- **Regular Motion**: Serve 16 court days before hearing
- **Contempt**: Personal service at least 10 days before
- **Reply Papers**: File 9 court days before hearing
- **Response Papers**: File 9 court days before hearing
